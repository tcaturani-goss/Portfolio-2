Plantiva Rebranding

Project Overview

Challenges

Plantiva is an immune supplement company that uses all-natural and plant-based ingredients to make each one of their products. They currently have branding established but would prefer their branding have a more updated and refined look.

They recently started selling many of their products on Amazon to boost sales and the number of consumers they reach. Since they are just now moving over to Amazon and have a lot of new products coming down the pipeline, they are looking to come out with a bang with a rebrand of the logo, brand colors, and labels.

Main Objectives

  • Rebrand Plantiva's colors, logo, and labels
  • Create coherent branding that aligns with Plantiva's current and future customer base

Project Scope

Graphical Elements, Branding

Tools Used

Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Canva

Role

Graphic Designer (Research, Digital Asset Design)

Team

Project Manager, Lead Designer, Marketing Team

Duration

Currently In Progress

Rebranding Process

Research

Research Plan

At the beginning of the rebranding process, it is essential to understand who the client is, the consumer, and the target audience they are trying to market to, and their competitors.

This is why having a solid research plan is crucial because it ensures that the rebranding process goes by smoothly. I have listed goals within my research plan that will help clarify key points in the rebranding process.

Research Goals

  • Understand the supplement market
  • Identify the consumer and target audience
  • Identify Plantiva's main competitors and how they cater to customers while pinpointing their strengths/weaknesses.

Market Research

Market research gives us a better idea of what market our target audience falls in and how to cater our designs to that market. It also helps with better understanding consumer personas and building them to reflect the ideal consumer.

Vitamin Supplement Market Trend

  • $35.7bn Vitamin & Supplement Manufacturing in the US Market Size in 2021. (IBISWorld)
  • The global dietary supplements market size was valued at USD 140.3 billion in 2020 and is expected to expand at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8.6% from 2021 to 2028. (Grand View Research)
  • nutritional supplement industry observed its highest growth in over 2 decades, with a 12.1% increase in 2020. (healthline)

Demographics

  • The market for plant protein ingredients is growing at a high rate; consumers are drifting away from animal proteins to plant-based protein. (MarketsAndMarkets)
  • The COVID-19 pandemic taught us all to prioritize our health. As such, there’s been a 50% reported increase in consumers seeking out immune-supporting supplements. (healthline)
  • Researchers have found that function-specific antioxidants, especially for boosting one’s immunity, are set to create new growth opportunities. (GlobeNewswire)

Online Supplement Marketing Trend

  • Since the pandemic occurred, there has been an enormous boost in online sales for supplements, especially when it comes to immune-boosting supplements.
  • According to Nutrition Business Journal, many of the consumers who switched to online supplement purchasing during the pandemic will continue to do so, with online supplement sales forecast to grow from $5 billion in 2019 to more than $10 billion in 2022. (glanbianutritionals)
  • The “immunity supplement” category saw tremendous growth on Amazon over the last two years, posting an astonishing 165% growth rate from 2019 to 2020 to reach $623 million in 2020 revenue. (Natural Products Insider)

Once I have refined and reviewed the personas of the target audience we are trying to reach, it is time to do competitive research to understand better how Plantiva's competition is connecting with customers and reaching the target audience. Knowing the strengths and weaknesses of the competition is a great way to gain an extra step ahead by catering to customers' needs/wants and going above and beyond the competitors.

Design

Mood Board

When it came to designing the mood board, we wanted to set a branding color palette of calm colors that would pair well with social media design backgrounds as well labeling in the future. One of our main goals with the new color palette was to have it correlate with the personas of our target audience. Hence the reason the bottom right image is a picture of "The Active Dad" enjoying a beach trip with his wife and children he adores. The top middle image is "The Lively Senior," who is happy to be living healthy for her family and glad to be enjoying time with them. Then we have a few images to resemble "The Healthy Millennial," one of her making an organic smoothy to pose with and share the recipe on social media or one of her working on her naturally grown plants which she prefers in any of her supplements. The new palette blends well with the vibrant, natural, and trustworthy image that Plantiva is looking to emanate.

Logos

The CEO wanted to see what a logo design with roots incorporated into it would look like, so I took this opportunity to make a logo that could be used as a secondary logo and explained the importance of having primary/secondary designs.

The project manager wanted to see what a line drawing of one of our ingredients would look like incorporated into a logo, so we decided to make mockup designs with astragalus leaves integrated into the designs.
